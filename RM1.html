The Current Landscape: A Mix of Promise and Concern

Previous research reveals a dual perspective among K-12 teachers regarding AI. We aim to understand how this reflects in Dehradun.

The Good

Efficiency & Engagement

Teachers see potential in AI for automating tasks like grading and creating personalised, engaging lessons for students.

The Bad

Dependence & Ethics

Major worries include students over-relying on AI, academic dishonesty, data privacy, and the changing role of the teacher.

What Matters

Context is Key

Factors like a teacher's age, gender, experience, and the subject they teach significantly influence their perspective on AI.

Charting a Clear Path: Four Core Objectives

To move beyond generalisations, our research is guided by four precise, actionable goals.

1. To Measure

Determine the actual level of AI understanding and attitudes among K-12 teachers in Dehradun.

2. To Identify

Pinpoint demographic factors (age, gender, experience, subject) that shape these perceptions.

3. To Analyse

Catalogue the primary hopes, specific concerns, and perceived barriers teachers face.

4. To Recommend

Translate findings into practical guidance for schools and policymakers on training strategies.

The Quantitative Lens: Structured Questionnaire

Distributed online (Google Form) or on paper.

Section 1: Demographics

Collects essential background information:

  • Age & Gender
  • Years of Experience
  • Subject(s) Taught
  • School Type (Public/Private)

Section 2: Perception Scales

Uses a 1-5 rating scale ("Strongly Disagree" to "Strongly Agree") to assess:

  • AI Literacy: "I feel I can clearly explain what AI is."
  • Perceived Usefulness: "AI will help me save time on grading."

Decoding the Data: Analysis (Part 1)

All quantitative data analysed using SPSS. Significance level: p < 0.05.

Primary Test 1: T-Test

What it does: Compares the average score on a question between two different groups.

Example Application:
